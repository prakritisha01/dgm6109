Phase 3b Term Project Sketches

This project examines my sleep dataset relationships. The information covers date, screen time before bed (minutes), bedtime, amount of sleep (hours), quality of sleep (rated on a 1-5 scale) and disturbances (sleep talking and snoring).

Sketch 1 - Scatterplot

This visualization is based on the method of a scatterplot that was practiced in class. It looks at the connection between screen time before bed and sleep quality in addition to adding the total sleep duration to enhance the analysis.

Hypothesis

As my screen time before bed increases, my sleep quality decreases. Additionally, higher screen time is also associated with fewer total sleep hours.

Data

  • Screen time before bed (minutes)
  • Sleep quality (1-5 scale)
  • Total sleep duration (hours)

Units and Range

  • Screen time: 15-155 minutes
  • Sleep quality: 1 (very poor) to 5 (very good)
  • Total sleep: 4 to 9 hours

Representation

  • Horizontal axis: Screen Time Before Bed (minutes)
  • Vertical axis: Sleep Quality (1–5 scale)
  • Shape type represents total sleep duration:
    • Upright triangle indicates more sleep
    • Inverted triangle indicates fewer hours slept
  • A dotted vertical reference line at 60 minutes highlights a screen-time threshold
  • The reference lines divide the graph into four quadrants: top left represents low screen time and high sleep quality, top right represents high screen time and high sleep quality, bottom left represents low screen time and low sleep quality, and bottom right represents high screen time and low sleep quality

Sketch 2 - Time-Based Heatmap

The visualization examines the variation of the quality of sleep between days according to the amount of time spent on the screen before bed. Rather than the plotting of sleep quality on an axis, it is presented in a color scale, thereby allowing patterns to rise over time.

Hypothesis

Days with higher screen time before bed are more likely to display lower sleep quality values across the observed period.

Data

  • Screen time before bed (minutes)
  • Date
  • Sleep Quality (1-5 scale)

Units and Range

  • Screen time: 15-155 minutes
  • Date: consecutive days in the observed period
  • Sleep 1uality: 1 (very poor) to 5 (very good)

Representation

  • Horizontal axis: Screen Time Before Bed (minutes)
  • Vertical axis: Date
  • Color intensity represents sleep quality, where darker shades indicate lower sleep quality and lighter shades indicate higher sleep quality

Sketch 3 - Sleep Pulse Timeline

This visualization is a pulse line of every night portrayed chronologically. The pulse provides a visual representation of the quality of sleep and incorporates the screen time and sleep disturbances into the same framework.

Hypothesis

Nights with higher screen time and the presence of sleep disturbances (sleep talking or snoring) are associated with lower sleep quality.

Data

  • Date
  • Screen time before bed (minutes)
  • Sleep quality (1-5 scale)
  • Sleep talking (yes/no)
  • Snoring (yes/no)

Units and Range

  • Screen time: 15-155 minutes
  • Sleep quality: 1-5
  • Disturbance factors: present or absent

Representation

  • Horizontal axis: Date (chronological order)
  • Vertical axis: Sleep Quality (1–5 scale)
  • Pulse height represents sleep quality:
    • Taller pulse = higher sleep quality
    • Shorter pulse = lower sleep quality
  • Screen time indicator:
    • A small heart symbol is placed at the top of the pulse for nights with low screen time (below 60 minutes)
    • Nights with higher screen time do not display the heart symbol
  • Sleep disturbances affect line style:
    • Solid line = no disturbance
    • Dotted line = sleep talking
    • Wavy line = snoring
    • Wavy dotted line = both sleep talking and snoring
