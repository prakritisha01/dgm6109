How Screen Time Before Bed Affects Sleep Quality

Question: Does sleep quality decrease on nights when screen time before bed is higher than 60 minutes?

About the Visualization

In this Scatterplot, the horizontal axis shows screen time and the quality of sleep is shown on the vertical axis. A red dashed line at 60 min helps seperate lower and higher screen-time nights.

Additional Data Shown

The triangle direction shows whether the total sleep was 7 hours or more. Night with 7 or more hours of sleep are depicted with upward triangles and nights with less than 7 hours of sleep are depicted with a downward triangle.

Patterns and Conclusion

Visualisation indicates that the number of nights, on which the duration of screen time is less than 60 minutes, are more frequently related to the ratings of 3, 4, or 5 on the scale of sleep quality. Within 60 minutes, additional points are noted to appear towards the bottom of the scale, particularly towards the ratings of 1, 2, and 3. Even in the case of more screen time, there are still a few exceptions, in which sleep quality is not dropped, and thus, the relationship is not ideal. In general, the figure indicates that the increased screen time before sleep is mostly associated with the worse sleep quality.
